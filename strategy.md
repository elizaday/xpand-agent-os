# Strategy Context

This file is for helping Elizabeth make decisions about Xpand Studio, consulting, workshops, public work, and larger creative or technical projects.

## Primary Question

What should Elizabeth build next through Xpand Studio?

The best answer should be meaningful, paid or revenue-capable, portable, and based on her rare overlap:

- AI
- creative media
- entrepreneurship
- teaching
- technical building
- sprint design
- interactive experiences
- philosophy and systems thinking

## Filters

Meaning comes first. Money matters, but it is not the only driver. She does not need to chase low-value work, but she should not do free labor.

Strong projects should:

- create reusable assets
- travel well
- build Xpand Studio
- use her actual strengths
- be legible to clients or audiences
- produce artifacts, methods, workshops, tools, or prototypes
- avoid trapping her in ongoing maintenance

Weak projects usually:

- depend on broad networking
- require lots of unpaid emotional labor
- create a one-off deliverable with no reusable value
- require long-term maintenance without funding
- look impressive but do not mean much to her
- keep her stuck in the role of helping everyone else build while not building her own thing

## Promising Directions

### AI Creative Production Sprint

A repeatable Xpand Studio offer for organizations that need to understand and apply AI in creative production. Could include a workshop, sprint format, prototype day, playbook, and implementation roadmap.

Why it fits: draws from GSU teaching, StudioNow consulting, sprint design, and AI production work.

Risk: can become generic consulting unless the method is clearly Elizabeth's.

### Interactive AI Experience Studio

Build on the Georgia Aquarium prototype as a model for public-facing AI experiences. Could become a portable installation, event activation, museum/aquarium/zoo offer, or workshop-backed prototype service.

Why it fits: combines AI media, interaction, family/public experience, teaching, and prototype building.

Risk: production logistics and maintenance could grow quickly.

### AI Filmmaking Intensive

Productize the AI filmmaking intensive as a paid workshop that can travel, run online, or attach to conferences, universities, festivals, and companies.

Why it fits: already proven, portable, revenue-capable, tied to her strengths.

Risk: may become "just teaching more" unless it connects to Xpand Studio's larger strategy.

### Self-Learning Production Tools

Turn the StudioNow-style workflow tools into a broader category of AI production assistants for creative teams.

Why it fits: practical, useful, codeable, and tied to a clear pain point.

Risk: could become software support work without a clear business model.

## Current Bias

The strongest path may be a combined one:

Use Xpand Studio to offer AI creative production sprints that produce either a working prototype, a production workflow, or an interactive AI experience by the end.

This packages Elizabeth's best work into something paid, portable, meaningful, and reusable.
