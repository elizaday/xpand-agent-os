# Elizabeth Strickler Profile

Elizabeth Strickler is a Principal Senior Lecturer at Georgia State University, jointly appointed in the Entrepreneurship and Innovation Institute and the Creative Media Industries Institute. She is Director of Media Entrepreneurship and Innovation at CMII, co-founded CMII, and built the media entrepreneurship program from scratch.

Her independent business is Xpand Studio, operated through elizaday LLC. Xpand Studio is the vehicle for consulting, speaking, workshops, AI filmmaking intensives, and independent projects outside GSU.

Elizabeth works at the intersection of AI, creative media, entrepreneurship, teaching, and production. A useful shorthand: she teaches creativity to business people and business to creatives.

## Background

- B.A. Philosophy, Boston College
- B.S. Computer Science, Georgia State University
- MFA Digital Filmmaking, Georgia State University
- Former programmer during the dotcom era
- Helped build GSU's internet infrastructure
- Trained filmmaker who now teaches and builds with AI media tools

Elizabeth is not learning to code through AI. She already has technical foundations. AI tools accelerate work she was already capable of doing.

## Working Style

Elizabeth moves fast, talks through messy ideas, and thinks across systems. She is comfortable with ambiguity, but she needs help turning many possible threads into a smaller set of serious choices.

She works mostly independently or with a small circle of trusted collaborators. She is more builder than networker. She is known for building programs, institutions, classes, tools, and experiences, not just producing isolated artifacts.

She likes sprint-based work, live prototypes, usable systems, and projects that can keep growing after the first version.

## Current Strategic Shape

Elizabeth is in a transition period. GSU remains a platform, identity, pension path, and institutional home. Xpand Studio is the independent vehicle for the next phase: consulting, workshops, talks, tools, and larger projects.

The central strategic question is what she should build next through Xpand Studio. The answer should satisfy meaning first, revenue second, portability third, and avoid creating a maintenance trap.

## Collaboration Notes

Help Elizabeth think clearly without flattening her voice. She does not need generic motivation. She needs useful structure, honest pushback, and artifacts she can actually use.
