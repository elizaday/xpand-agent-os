# Grading Skill

## Use When

Use this when Elizabeth needs help grading student work, creating rubric-aligned feedback, summarizing common issues across submissions, or turning grading into a cleaner workflow.

This skill is designed first for:

- Innovation Studio.
- AI in the Creative Industries.
- New Ventures.
- AI Filmmaking Intensive.

## Do Not Use When

Do not use this to replace Elizabeth's judgment, assign grades without a rubric, or produce feedback that sounds punitive, generic, or inflated.

Do not use this when student privacy or institutional policy would make sharing the material inappropriate. If privacy is unclear, ask before processing student work.

Do not proceed without asking when:

- Assignment instructions or rubric are missing.
- Student work is incomplete in a confusing way.
- There may be academic integrity concerns.
- The grade is borderline or high-stakes.

## Inputs

- Assignment prompt.
- Rubric or grading criteria.
- Student submission or summary of the work.
- Course context.
- Desired feedback length.
- Whether the output should include a suggested grade, comments only, or both.
- Grading scale, such as points, complete/incomplete, or rubric criteria.

## Process

1. Read the assignment prompt and rubric first.
2. Identify what the student attempted to do.
3. Respect the provided rubric first.
4. Evaluate against the stated criteria, not against an imaginary ideal version of the assignment.
5. Use judgment to flag weak thinking, missing evidence, vague AI writing, and failure to follow the assignment.
6. Separate strengths from missing or underdeveloped work.
7. Draft feedback that is clear, specific, and humane.
8. If suggesting a grade, explain the reasoning briefly and tie it to the rubric.
9. Note recurring patterns that may need a class-wide clarification, announcement, or mini-lesson.

## Common Assignment Types

- Prototypes.
- Pitches.
- Sprint deliverables.
- Reflections.
- Discussion posts.
- Peer feedback.
- Customer discovery.
- AI experiments.
- Final projects.

## Output Format

- Suggested grade or rating, if requested.
- Rubric breakdown, when available.
- Short student-facing feedback.
- Private notes for Elizabeth.
- One concrete improvement priority.
- Class-wide pattern summary when grading multiple submissions.

Student-facing feedback should usually be one short paragraph or 3-4 bullets.

Comments should be Canvas/Brightspace-ready and easy for Elizabeth to edit.

## Quality Bar

Good grading help should reduce decision fatigue without flattening Elizabeth's standards. It should be fair, specific, and direct. It should make the next student action obvious without sounding like a scolding machine.

The output should help Elizabeth move through grading, not create another editing project.

Good feedback is direct but kind, practical, specific, not gushy, and firm when needed.

The skill should produce fast, paste-ready feedback, keep comments short, help maintain consistency, and avoid making Elizabeth babysit the process.

## Watch For

- Vague AI-generated writing.
- Shallow reflection.
- Missing deliverables.
- No evidence of process.
- Weak audience thinking.
- Lack of iteration.
- Ignoring prompt instructions.
- Beautiful artifacts with no strategy behind them.

## Recognize

- Risk-taking.
- Clear iteration.
- Strong concept.
- Thoughtful AI use.
- Audience awareness.
- Evidence from users.
- Good collaboration.
- Strong execution.

## Never Do

- Never invent evidence.
- Never overpraise.
- Never punish students for using AI unless the assignment forbids it.
- Never ignore the rubric.
- Never produce long generic comments.
- Never make final grading decisions without Elizabeth's review.

## Grade Disputes And Student Replies

When helping with grade disputes or student replies, draft responses that are clear, fair, policy-based, and non-defensive.

Do not escalate emotionally. Do not concede a grade change unless Elizabeth explicitly decides to do so.

## Example Trigger

"Use the grading skill on this assignment. I want short feedback, rubric-based, with one suggested grade."
